How to Choose a Cash Back Card

Key factors to consider before you apply.

1

Know Your Spending Categories

Review your last three months of credit card or bank statements and identify your top spending categories. If groceries or gas dominate, a tiered card with bonus rates in those categories will outperform a flat-rate card. If your spending is spread evenly, a simple 2% everywhere card is likely the better choice.

2

Compare Annual Fee vs Rewards

Most cash back cards charge no annual fee, but some premium options like the Amex Blue Cash Preferred charge $95 for higher earning rates. Calculate your expected annual cash back at the higher rate and subtract the fee. If the net rewards still exceed what a free card would earn, the fee is worth paying.

3

Check the Redemption Options

The best cash back cards let you redeem rewards as a statement credit, direct deposit, or check with no minimums. Some cards restrict redemptions to gift cards or require a minimum balance before you can cash out. Make sure the card you choose offers a redemption method that works for you.

Cash Back Credit Card FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about cash back cards.

What is a cash back credit card?
A cash back credit card returns a percentage of your spending as a statement credit, direct deposit, or check. Rates typically range from 1% to 6% depending on the card and spending category. Cash back is one of the simplest forms of credit card rewards because the value is straightforward and does not require point conversions or travel bookings.
What is the best cash back credit card right now?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash card is a top choice for its flat 2% cash back on all purchases with no annual fee. For higher category rates, the Amex Blue Cash Preferred earns 6% at U.S. supermarkets and the Citi Custom Cash earns 5% on your top spending category automatically.
How do rotating category cash back cards work?
Rotating category cards like the Discover it Cash Back offer 5% cash back in categories that change every quarter, such as gas stations, restaurants, or Amazon. You must activate the bonus each quarter to earn the higher rate, and there is usually a spending cap of $1,500 per quarter at the 5% rate. All other purchases earn 1%.
Is 2% cash back good?
A flat 2% cash back rate is considered excellent for a no-annual-fee card and outperforms most rewards cards on non-bonus spending. Over a year, someone spending $2,000 per month would earn $480 in cash back. It is especially valuable as a default card for purchases that do not fall into a higher bonus category on another card.
Do cash back cards have annual fees?
Many of the best cash back cards have no annual fee at all, including the Wells Fargo Active Cash and Citi Custom Cash. Some premium cash back cards like the Amex Blue Cash Preferred charge an annual fee of $95 but offer higher earning rates that can easily offset the cost for heavy grocery spenders.
Can I get cash back as a direct deposit?
Most major cash back cards let you redeem rewards as a statement credit, direct deposit to a bank account, or a check. Some cards also allow you to redeem for gift cards or to apply rewards at checkout. Statement credits and direct deposits are typically the best value redemption options.
What credit score do I need for a cash back card?
Most top cash back cards require a good to excellent credit score of 670 or higher. The best offers with the highest earning rates generally require scores of 700 or above. If your score is below 670, consider a secured cash back card or a student cash back card to start building your rewards history.
Should I get a flat rate or category cash back card?
A flat-rate card is best if your spending is spread evenly across many categories or you prefer simplicity. A category card is better if you spend heavily in specific areas like groceries, dining, or gas. Many people use both types together, using the category card for bonus spending and the flat-rate card for everything else.
