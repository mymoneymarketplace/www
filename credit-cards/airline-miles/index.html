How to Choose an Airline Miles Card

Key factors to consider before picking your card.

1

Pick Your Airline or Stay Flexible

If you consistently fly one airline and value perks like free bags and priority boarding, an airline-specific card is your best bet. If you prefer booking the cheapest flight regardless of carrier, choose a card with transferable miles that work across multiple airline partners for maximum redemption flexibility.

2

Evaluate the Welcome Bonus

Airline card welcome bonuses can be worth several hundred dollars in free flights. Compare the bonus amount against the spending requirement and timeline. A 60,000-mile bonus might require $3,000 in spending over three months. Make sure you can meet the threshold with your normal purchases to avoid overspending.

3

Calculate the True Cost

Annual fees on airline cards range from $0 to $395 or more. Add up the value of every perk you would actually use, including free bags, lounge access, travel credits, and companion passes. Subtract the annual fee to determine your net value. A higher fee card can deliver far more value than a free card if you fly enough to use the benefits.

Airline Miles Card FAQs

Common questions about earning and using airline miles.

What is the best airline miles credit card?
The Capital One Venture X stands out as the best overall airline miles card thanks to its 75,000-mile welcome bonus, $300 annual travel credit, Priority Pass lounge access, and 2x miles on every purchase. It offers strong value for frequent and occasional travelers alike.
How do airline miles credit cards work?
Airline miles cards earn miles on your everyday purchases. These miles can be redeemed for flights, upgrades, and other travel expenses with specific airlines or through flexible travel portals. Some cards earn miles with a single airline while others earn transferable miles you can move to multiple airline partners.
Are airline credit cards worth the annual fee?
Airline credit cards are worth the fee if you fly frequently enough to use the perks. Benefits like free checked bags, priority boarding, and companion certificates can easily offset a $95 to $250 annual fee if you take just a few flights per year with that airline.
Can I transfer miles between airline programs?
You cannot transfer miles directly between airline programs, but cards with flexible points currencies like Capital One, Chase, and Amex allow you to transfer points to multiple airline partners. This gives you more options when searching for award flights and better redemption values.
What credit score do I need for an airline miles card?
Most airline miles credit cards require good to excellent credit, typically a score of 670 or higher. Premium airline cards from issuers like Amex and Chase usually require scores of 700 or above. Check your credit score before applying to improve your approval odds.
Do airline miles expire?
Expiration policies vary by airline. Most major US airline programs no longer expire miles as long as your account remains open. However, some international airlines still expire miles after 18 to 36 months of inactivity. Check your specific airline program terms for details.
Should I get an airline card or a general travel card?
Choose an airline-specific card if you are loyal to one airline and fly with them regularly. The airline perks like free bags and priority boarding add significant value. Choose a general travel card with transferable points if you prefer flexibility and want to book the best deal across multiple airlines.
How do I maximize airline miles from credit cards?
Use your airline card for all purchases to accumulate miles faster, especially in bonus categories like dining and travel. Meet welcome bonus spending requirements promptly. Book flights through the airline to earn both card miles and frequent flyer miles. Use airline shopping portals for online purchases to earn additional bonus miles.
