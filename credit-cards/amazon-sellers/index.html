What to Know

How We Picked the Best Cards for Amazon Sellers

We compared annual fees, welcome bonuses, category earn rates, and fine print across every mainstream issuer, then filtered to cards whose bonus categories align with how amazon sellers typically spend. Every card on this list earns at least 2% effective cash-back return at realistic monthly spend.

Match the Card to Real Spending, Not the Marketing

The best card for amazon sellers is not the one with the flashiest welcome bonus -- it is the one that earns the most on your actual monthly spend. Pull up the last three months of statements, sum spend by category, and pick the card whose multiplier aligns with your biggest line items. If your spending is spread evenly, a flat-rate 2% card wins.

Responsible Use and Credit-Score Impact

Credit cards help your score when you pay the full balance every month and keep utilization below 30% of your limit. They hurt your score when you carry balances at 20%+ APR or miss payments. Set up autopay for at least the minimum on day one of the card to protect your payment history, then aim to pay the full statement balance each cycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for Amazon FBA inventory purchases?
The Ink Business Preferred is the best overall card for Amazon FBA inventory purchases because it earns 3x points on shipping -- a massive expense for FBA sellers -- and 3x on advertising including Amazon PPC campaigns. For sellers who want a flat rate on all inventory, the Capital One Spark Cash Plus earns unlimited 2% on every purchase with no cap.
Should Amazon sellers use business credit cards?
Yes, business credit cards are nearly essential for Amazon sellers. They separate personal and business expenses for cleaner tax filing, typically offer higher credit limits needed for inventory purchases, and provide business expense tracking tools. Most Amazon sellers operate as LLCs or sole proprietors, making them eligible for business cards.
What is the best card for Amazon PPC advertising spend?
The Ink Business Preferred earns 3x points on advertising purchases made with social media sites and search engines, which includes Amazon PPC campaigns. For sellers spending heavily on Sponsored Products and Sponsored Brands ads, this can generate significant rewards on a major business expense.
Can I use credit cards for cash flow between Amazon payouts?
Yes, many Amazon sellers use credit cards strategically to bridge the gap between inventory purchases and Amazon payouts, which arrive on a 2-week cycle. A card with a 0% intro APR period can be especially helpful for new sellers building inventory. Just ensure you can pay the balance when the promotional period ends to avoid high interest charges.
What are the best high-limit cards for large inventory orders?
The Capital One Spark Cash Plus is a charge card with no preset spending limit, making it ideal for large inventory orders. The Amex Business Gold also handles large purchases well with its $150K spending cap for 4x rewards. Both cards are designed for businesses with variable and potentially high monthly spending.
Is the Amazon Business Prime Card worth it for sellers?
The Amazon Business Prime Card is worth it if you buy a significant amount of supplies directly from Amazon, as it earns 5% back on Amazon and Whole Foods purchases. However, most inventory for resale comes from suppliers and manufacturers, not Amazon itself. Consider pairing it with a general business card for non-Amazon purchases.
What are the best cards for shipping and FBA fees?
The Ink Business Preferred earns 3x on shipping purchases, making it the top choice for sellers with significant shipping costs. For FBA fees specifically, these are charged directly by Amazon and earn standard rates on most cards unless you use the Amazon Business Prime Card for 5% back on Amazon purchases.
How should Amazon sellers manage cash flow with credit cards?
Time your inventory purchases to align with your credit card billing cycle so you have maximum float before payment is due. Pay your balance in full each statement to avoid interest that would erase any rewards earned. Consider a card with a 0% intro APR for your first major inventory build, and always maintain a cash reserve to cover the credit card payment even if Amazon payouts are delayed.
