What to Know

How We Picked the Best Cards for Restaurant Owners

We compared annual fees, welcome bonuses, category earn rates, and fine print across every mainstream issuer, then filtered to cards whose bonus categories align with how restaurant owners typically spend. Every card on this list earns at least 2% effective cash-back return at realistic monthly spend.

Match the Card to Real Spending, Not the Marketing

The best card for restaurant owners is not the one with the flashiest welcome bonus -- it is the one that earns the most on your actual monthly spend. Pull up the last three months of statements, sum spend by category, and pick the card whose multiplier aligns with your biggest line items. If your spending is spread evenly, a flat-rate 2% card wins.

Responsible Use and Credit-Score Impact

Credit cards help your score when you pay the full balance every month and keep utilization below 30% of your limit. They hurt your score when you carry balances at 20%+ APR or miss payments. Set up autopay for at least the minimum on day one of the card to protect your payment history, then aim to pay the full statement balance each cycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best business credit card for restaurant owners?
The American Express Business Gold Card is the best overall credit card for restaurant owners because it automatically earns 4x points on your top two spending categories each billing cycle. For most restaurants, those categories will be food distributors and shipping, earning significant rewards on $10,000-$50,000+ in monthly ingredient purchases without any manual category selection.
Should restaurants use business credit cards for food purchases?
Absolutely. Restaurant owners who put food distributor invoices on a rewards business credit card can earn thousands of dollars annually in cash back or points. A restaurant spending $30,000 per month on food supplies could earn $3,600-$7,200+ per year depending on the card. Business cards also provide expense tracking, employee cards for managers, and separation between personal and business finances for tax purposes.
What are the best cards with employee card options for restaurants?
The American Express Business Gold Card offers employee cards at no additional cost, making it ideal for restaurants where managers and purchasers need to make supply runs. The Ink Business Preferred also provides free employee cards with the ability to set spending limits. The Ramp Business Card goes further with built-in spending controls per employee and automatic receipt capture.
How much can restaurants earn in credit card rewards?
Restaurant reward earnings depend on monthly spend volume. A small restaurant spending $20,000 per month could earn $4,800-$9,600 annually with the right card. A larger operation spending $50,000-$100,000+ monthly could earn $12,000-$24,000+ per year. The key is choosing a card that maximizes rewards on your highest-volume categories like food distributors, shipping, and utilities.
What is the best no annual fee card for small restaurants?
The Chase Ink Business Cash is the best no-fee option for smaller restaurants. It earns 5% on office supplies, internet, and phone services -- covering POS system subscriptions and restaurant internet -- plus 2% on gas and dining for the first $25,000 annually. For restaurants that want simplicity with no fee, the Ramp Business Card offers unlimited 1.5% cash back with built-in expense management tools.
What are the best cards for new restaurant startups?
New restaurant owners should consider the Ink Business Preferred for its 100,000-point welcome bonus worth $1,250+ to help offset startup costs. The Ramp Business Card is another strong option for startups because it has no annual fee, provides built-in expense management, and offers spending controls per employee from day one. Both cards help establish business credit history essential for future financing.
What is the best card for restaurant advertising spend?
The Chase Ink Business Preferred earns 3x points on social media advertising, Google Ads, and other online advertising purchases. For restaurants spending $1,000-$5,000+ monthly on digital marketing, delivery app promotions, and local advertising, this translates to significant rewards. The Amex Business Gold may also earn 4x on advertising if it falls into your top two spending categories.
Should restaurant owners have multiple business cards?
Many successful restaurant owners use a two-card strategy to maximize rewards. A primary card like the Amex Business Gold handles the highest-volume categories at 4x, while a secondary flat-rate card like the Capital One Spark Cash Plus earns 2% on everything else the primary card does not bonus. This combination ensures every dollar spent earns the highest possible rate across all restaurant expense categories.
