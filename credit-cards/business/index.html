How to Choose a Business Card

Key factors to consider before you apply.

1

Map Your Business Expenses

Review your business spending over the past year to identify your largest expense categories. If you spend heavily on office supplies and internet, the Ink Business Cash will maximize your rewards. If your biggest costs are shipping and travel, the Ink Business Preferred is a better match. Choose a card that aligns with where your money actually goes.

2

Consider the Welcome Bonus

Business card welcome bonuses can be worth $750 to $1,250 or more in value. Calculate whether you can meet the minimum spending requirement with your normal business expenses. A bonus worth $1,000 can effectively cover the annual fee for multiple years, making even fee-based cards a strong value in the first year.

3

Evaluate Employee Card Features

If you have employees who make purchases for the business, look for cards that offer free employee cards with individual spending limits. This simplifies expense management, allows you to earn rewards on all business spending, and provides detailed reporting that makes bookkeeping and tax preparation much easier.

Business Credit Card FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about business cards.

What is a business credit card?
A business credit card is designed for business owners and allows you to separate personal and business expenses, earn rewards on business spending, and access higher credit limits. Business cards often offer bonus categories tailored to common business expenses like office supplies, internet, phone, shipping, and advertising.
Do I need an LLC or corporation to get a business card?
No, you do not need a formal business entity to qualify for a business credit card. Sole proprietors, freelancers, and independent contractors can apply using their Social Security number and personal information. You can use your legal name as the business name and report any income from self-employment, side hustles, or gig work.
What is the best business credit card right now?
The Ink Business Cash is the top pick for most small businesses thanks to its 5% cash back on office supplies, internet, and phone services, with no annual fee. For businesses with higher spending, the Ink Business Preferred offers a 100,000-point welcome bonus and 3x on travel and shipping, making it ideal for businesses that ship products or travel frequently.
Do business credit cards affect my personal credit?
Most business credit card issuers perform a hard inquiry on your personal credit when you apply, which temporarily impacts your score. Once approved, most issuers report the account to business credit bureaus rather than personal ones. However, if you miss payments or default, the issuer may report the delinquency to personal credit bureaus as well.
What are the benefits of a business credit card over a personal card?
Business credit cards offer higher credit limits, bonus rewards on business-related spending categories, employee card management, detailed expense tracking and reporting, and the ability to build business credit history. They also help you maintain a clean separation between personal and business finances for tax and accounting purposes.
Can I use a business card for personal purchases?
While most issuers do not explicitly prohibit personal purchases on a business card, mixing personal and business spending defeats the purpose of having a separate business card. It complicates your accounting and tax preparation and could weaken the liability protection that comes with keeping business and personal finances separate.
How do business card welcome bonuses work?
Business card welcome bonuses work the same as personal cards. You must meet a minimum spending requirement within a specified time frame, typically 3 months, to earn the bonus. Business card bonuses tend to be larger than personal cards, with some offering 75,000 to 100,000 points or $750 to $1,000 in cash back.
What expenses earn the most rewards on business cards?
The highest-earning categories on business cards typically include office supplies, internet and phone services, shipping, advertising, travel, and dining. Cards like the Ink Business Cash earn 5% on office supplies, internet, and phone, while the Amex Business Gold earns 4x on the two categories where your business spends the most each month.
