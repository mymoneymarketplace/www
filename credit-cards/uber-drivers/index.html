What to Know

How We Picked the Best Cards for Uber Drivers

We compared annual fees, welcome bonuses, category earn rates, and fine print across every mainstream issuer, then filtered to cards whose bonus categories align with how uber drivers typically spend. Every card on this list earns at least 2% effective cash-back return at realistic monthly spend.

Match the Card to Real Spending, Not the Marketing

The best card for uber drivers is not the one with the flashiest welcome bonus -- it is the one that earns the most on your actual monthly spend. Pull up the last three months of statements, sum spend by category, and pick the card whose multiplier aligns with your biggest line items. If your spending is spread evenly, a flat-rate 2% card wins.

Responsible Use and Credit-Score Impact

Credit cards help your score when you pay the full balance every month and keep utilization below 30% of your limit. They hurt your score when you carry balances at 20%+ APR or miss payments. Set up autopay for at least the minimum on day one of the card to protect your payment history, then aim to pay the full statement balance each cycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best gas card for Uber drivers?
The Costco Anywhere Visa by Citi is the best gas card for Uber drivers, earning 4% cash back at all gas stations on up to $7,000 per year in purchases. For drivers who spend $400-$500 per month on fuel, that translates to $192-$240 per year in gas rewards alone. The PenFed Platinum Rewards is a strong alternative with uncapped 5x points at gas stations for very high-mileage drivers.
Should rideshare drivers get a business credit card?
Yes, rideshare drivers are classified as independent contractors and can apply for business credit cards as sole proprietors. A business card helps separate personal and Uber expenses for cleaner Schedule C tax filing. However, if your driving income is part-time or supplemental, a personal card with strong gas rewards may be simpler and equally rewarding.
Can Uber drivers deduct credit card fees on taxes?
Uber drivers cannot deduct credit card annual fees as a business expense unless the card is used exclusively for business purposes. However, any annual fee on a business credit card used solely for rideshare expenses is fully deductible on Schedule C. Interest charges on business purchases may also be deductible. Consult a tax professional for guidance specific to your situation.
What is the best card for car maintenance expenses?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on all car maintenance expenses including oil changes, tire rotations, brake repairs, and car washes. Since auto repair shops do not fall into bonus categories on most cards, a flat-rate 2% card maximizes earnings on these essential rideshare costs. Cell phone protection is a bonus for protecting the phone you use for the Uber app.
How much can Uber drivers earn in credit card rewards?
A full-time Uber driver spending $5,000 per year on gas, $2,000 on car maintenance, and $1,500 on meals can earn $500 to $1,000 or more annually depending on the card. Using the Costco Anywhere Visa for gas (4% = $200) and the Chase Freedom Unlimited for dining (3% = $45) with a flat-rate card for everything else maximizes total rewards across all rideshare expenses.
What is the best no annual fee card for rideshare drivers?
The Citi Custom Cash is the best no-fee card for rideshare drivers because it automatically earns 5% on your top spending category each billing cycle. If gas is your biggest expense (which it is for most Uber drivers), you earn 5% on fuel purchases up to $500 per cycle with zero effort and no annual fee. The Wells Fargo Active Cash is a close second with reliable 2% on everything.
Should I use a separate card for Uber expenses?
Using a dedicated card for all Uber-related expenses simplifies tax preparation significantly. When every charge on the card is a business expense, you can download the annual statement and hand it directly to your accountant or use it for Schedule C deductions. This is especially valuable during tax season when you need to separate deductible mileage, gas, maintenance, and phone costs from personal spending.
What is the best card for part-time Uber drivers?
Part-time Uber drivers who spend less on gas should consider the Wells Fargo Active Cash for its simple 2% on everything -- gas, maintenance, car washes, and meals. Since part-time drivers may not hit the spending thresholds needed to maximize category bonuses, a flat-rate card provides consistent value without requiring high volume in any single category.
