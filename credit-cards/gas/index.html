How to Choose a Gas Credit Card

Key factors to consider for maximum fuel savings.

1

Calculate Your Monthly Gas Spending

Review your last three months of gas station receipts to determine your average monthly fuel expense. This helps you identify whether a card with a spending cap will limit your rewards or if you need an uncapped option. Drivers who spend over $300 per month on gas benefit most from high-rate cards like the PenFed Platinum Rewards.

2

Check for Spending Caps

Many gas cards limit the bonus rate to a certain dollar amount per quarter or year. The Costco Visa caps at $7,000 annually, while the Citi Custom Cash caps at $500 per billing cycle. Make sure your gas spending fits within the cap to maximize your rewards rate on every fill-up.

3

Pair with Everyday Spending Cards

The best approach is to use a dedicated gas card at the pump and a flat-rate or category card for other purchases. This strategy lets you earn the highest rate on gas while still getting solid rewards on groceries, dining, and general spending with a complementary card in your wallet.

Gas Credit Card FAQs

Common questions about gas rewards cards.

What is the best credit card for gas?
The Costco Anywhere Visa by Citi is the best gas credit card, earning 4% cash back at gas stations on up to $7,000 per year. If you do not have a Costco membership, the PenFed Platinum Rewards card earns 5x points at any gas station with no spending cap.
How much can I save with a gas credit card?
If you spend $200 per month on gas and earn 4% back, you save $96 per year. At 5% back, you save $120. Over several years, these savings add up significantly, especially for commuters or families with multiple vehicles filling up regularly.
Do gas credit cards work at all gas stations?
Most gas credit cards earn bonus rewards at any gas station, including major chains and independent stations. Some cards earn bonuses only at specific brands. Check the card terms to confirm whether your preferred stations qualify for the bonus earning rate.
Is there a gas card with no annual fee?
Yes, several gas cards have no annual fee. The Bank of America Customized Cash Rewards card earns 3% on gas with no annual cost, and the Citi Custom Cash earns up to 5% on gas if it is your top spending category each billing cycle, also with no annual fee.
Can I earn gas rewards on EV charging?
Most gas credit cards define gas station purchases by the merchant category code. Some EV charging stations are coded as gas stations and would earn the bonus rate, while others are coded differently. Check with your card issuer for specific merchant category details.
What is the difference between cash back and points for gas?
Cash back on gas gives you a straightforward dollar amount returned to your statement or bank account. Points earned on gas purchases may have variable value depending on how you redeem them. Cash back is simpler, while points can sometimes offer higher value if redeemed for travel.
Do gas cards have spending caps?
Some gas cards cap the bonus earning rate at a certain spending threshold. For example, the Costco Anywhere Visa caps the 4% gas rate at $7,000 per year. After reaching the cap, purchases earn at the base rate. Check each card for specific cap details before applying.
Should I get a gas card or a flat-rate cash back card?
A gas card is better if fuel is one of your largest monthly expenses and you want to maximize savings at the pump. A flat-rate 2% card is better if your spending is spread across many categories. You can also pair a gas card with a flat-rate card for the best of both approaches.
