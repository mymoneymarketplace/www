How to Choose a Hotel Credit Card

Key factors to consider before picking your card.

1

Pick Your Preferred Hotel Brand

Review where you stay most often. If you consistently book at Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, or IHG properties, a brand-specific card will earn you the highest points rates and hotel-specific perks like elite status and free nights. If you stay at various brands, a flexible card like the Chase Sapphire Preferred gives you transfer options to multiple hotel programs.

2

Value the Free Night Certificate

Many hotel cards offer a free night certificate each year on your card anniversary. This single benefit can offset or exceed the annual fee on its own. Compare the certificate point cap to the properties you typically book to ensure the free night works at hotels you want to stay at rather than only budget locations.

3

Consider Elite Status Benefits

Automatic elite status through a credit card provides room upgrades, late checkout, free breakfast at select brands, and bonus points on paid stays. If you stay more than a few nights per year, these perks add significant value beyond the points you earn on purchases and can enhance every stay without additional effort.

Hotel Credit Card FAQs

Common questions about hotel cards and rewards.

What is the best hotel credit card?
The Chase Sapphire Preferred is the best overall hotel card because its points transfer to Hyatt, Marriott, and IHG at strong ratios. If you are loyal to one brand, the Marriott Bonvoy Boundless or Hilton Honors Surpass offer higher earning rates within their respective programs.
How do hotel credit cards earn free nights?
Hotel credit cards earn points on purchases that you redeem for free nights. Many cards also offer a free night certificate annually just for holding the card. The number of points needed per night varies by hotel brand, property, and season.
Do hotel cards give you elite status?
Yes, many hotel credit cards provide automatic elite status as a cardholder benefit. For example, the World of Hyatt card grants Discoverist status, while certain Marriott cards offer Gold or Silver status. Elite status provides perks like room upgrades, late checkout, and bonus points on stays.
Are hotel card annual fees worth paying?
Hotel card annual fees are worth paying if you stay at hotels several times per year. A single free night certificate can often cover the annual fee. Add in elite status perks, bonus points, and travel insurance, and the value adds up quickly for regular hotel guests.
Can I use hotel points at any hotel?
Hotel points are generally restricted to the hotel brand that issued them. Marriott points work at Marriott properties, Hilton points at Hilton properties, and so on. Cards with transferable points like Chase Sapphire offer more flexibility since you can transfer to multiple hotel loyalty programs.
What credit score do I need for a hotel card?
Most hotel credit cards require a credit score of 670 or higher for approval. Premium hotel cards from Amex and Chase typically prefer scores of 700 or above. Check your credit score and review the card requirements before applying.
Should I get a hotel card or a general travel card?
Get a hotel card if you are loyal to one brand and stay frequently. The bonus earning rates and brand-specific perks add up fast. Choose a general travel card if you prefer booking across different hotel chains and want the flexibility of transferable points.
How do hotel free night certificates work?
Free night certificates are issued annually on your card anniversary. Each certificate can be used for one night at an eligible property within the hotel brand, usually up to a certain point value. You must book directly with the hotel to redeem the certificate and it typically cannot be combined with other promotions.
