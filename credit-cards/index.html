How to Choose the Right Credit Card

Three key factors to consider before you apply.

1

Match the Card to Your Spending

Review your monthly spending to identify where most of your money goes. If you spend heavily on groceries and gas, look for a card with bonus categories in those areas. A flat-rate cash back card is a solid alternative if your spending is spread across many categories. The goal is to earn the highest rewards on purchases you already make without changing your habits.

2

Consider the Welcome Bonus

Many credit cards offer a welcome bonus worth hundreds of dollars when you meet a minimum spending requirement in the first few months. These bonuses can be the single most valuable feature of a new card, so pay close attention to the offer amount and the spending threshold. Make sure you can meet the requirement with your normal spending to avoid unnecessary purchases just to hit the target.

3

Watch the Annual Fee

Annual fees range from $0 to over $500 depending on the card tier. A fee is worth paying only when the total value of the rewards, credits, and perks clearly exceeds the cost. Add up every benefit you would realistically use over a year and compare that total to the fee. If the math does not work in your favor, there are plenty of excellent no-annual-fee options available.

Credit Card FAQs

Answers to the most common credit card questions.

What is the best credit card right now?
The best credit card depends on your spending habits and goals. For everyday spending, a cash back card that earns 2% or more on all purchases is a strong choice. Travel enthusiasts should consider a travel rewards card with a generous welcome bonus. Compare cards in our category listings above to find the best fit for your needs.
How do I choose a credit card?
Start by identifying where you spend the most money each month, such as groceries, gas, dining, or travel. Then look for cards that offer the highest rewards in those categories. Consider the annual fee, interest rate, welcome bonus, and any additional perks like purchase protection or travel insurance before applying.
What credit score do I need for a credit card?
Most premium rewards cards require a credit score of 670 or higher. Cards in the good-to-excellent range typically start at 700+. If your score is below 670, consider a secured credit card or a credit builder card designed to help you establish or improve your credit history over time.
Cash back vs points -- which is better?
Cash back is simpler and provides straightforward value, making it ideal for people who want no-hassle rewards. Points and miles can deliver higher value per dollar when redeemed for travel through transfer partners, but they require more effort to maximize. Choose cash back for simplicity or points if you travel frequently and enjoy optimizing redemptions.
Are annual fee cards worth it?
Annual fee cards are worth it when the value of the rewards, perks, and benefits exceeds the cost of the fee. For example, a card with a $95 fee that offers $200 in travel credits and lounge access provides clear net value. Calculate the total value of all benefits you would actually use and compare it to the annual fee before deciding.
How many credit cards should I have?
There is no single right number, but most financial experts suggest having two to four cards to maximize rewards across different spending categories. Having multiple cards can also improve your credit utilization ratio, which helps your credit score. Only carry as many cards as you can manage responsibly without missing payments or overspending.
Will applying for a credit card hurt my credit score?
Applying for a credit card triggers a hard inquiry on your credit report, which typically lowers your score by 5 to 10 points temporarily. The impact fades within a few months and disappears from your report after two years. Avoid applying for multiple cards in a short period, as several hard inquiries close together can have a larger effect.
How do I maximize credit card rewards?
Use the right card for each purchase category to earn the highest reward rate possible. Pay your balance in full every month to avoid interest charges that erase your rewards value. Take advantage of welcome bonuses by meeting the minimum spending requirement, and review your card benefits regularly to make sure you are using all available perks.
