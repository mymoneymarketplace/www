How to Choose a Travel Card

Key factors to consider before you apply.

1

Evaluate the Welcome Bonus

Travel card welcome bonuses can be worth $500 to over $1,000 in travel value. Compare the bonus amount, the spending requirement to earn it, and the time frame. A larger bonus is valuable only if you can meet the minimum spend with your normal purchases without overspending just to hit the threshold.

2

Check Transfer Partners

The ability to transfer points to airline and hotel loyalty programs is what separates great travel cards from average ones. Look for cards with transfer partners that include airlines you actually fly and hotels where you stay. Flexible transfer programs from Chase, Amex, and Capital One offer the most versatility.

3

Factor in Travel Perks

Beyond earning rates, travel cards offer perks like airport lounge access, travel insurance, TSA PreCheck or Global Entry credits, and no foreign transaction fees. Add up the value of perks you would actually use and include them in your annual fee calculation to determine the true cost of the card.

Travel Rewards Card FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about travel cards.

What is a travel rewards credit card?
A travel rewards credit card earns points or miles on your everyday purchases that can be redeemed for flights, hotels, car rentals, and other travel expenses. Many travel cards also offer perks like airport lounge access, travel insurance, and no foreign transaction fees that make traveling more affordable and convenient.
What is the best travel credit card right now?
The Chase Sapphire Preferred is widely considered the best overall travel card for its 60,000-point welcome bonus, 3x earning on dining and travel, flexible point transfers to over a dozen airline and hotel partners, and a reasonable $95 annual fee. For premium travelers, the Capital One Venture X offers even more value with lounge access and a $300 annual travel credit.
Are travel credit cards worth the annual fee?
Travel cards are worth the annual fee if you travel at least a few times per year and can take advantage of the included perks. Cards like the Capital One Venture X charge $395 but include $300 in travel credits, lounge access, and a 75,000-mile bonus that can be worth over $1,000 in travel. Calculate the total value of perks you will actually use and compare it to the fee.
How do travel card points work?
Travel card points are earned as a multiple of your spending, such as 2x or 3x per dollar in certain categories. You can redeem points through the card issuer travel portal at a fixed rate or transfer them to airline and hotel loyalty programs where they can be worth significantly more. Transfer partners are the key to maximizing the value of flexible travel points.
What credit score do I need for a travel card?
Most premium travel rewards cards require a good to excellent credit score of 700 or higher. Some mid-tier travel cards may accept scores as low as 670. A strong credit history with on-time payments and low credit utilization will give you the best chances of approval for the top travel cards.
Do travel cards charge foreign transaction fees?
Most travel rewards cards do not charge foreign transaction fees, which is one of their key benefits for international travelers. This saves you the typical 3% fee that many other cards charge on purchases made outside the United States. Always confirm the foreign transaction fee policy before taking a card abroad.
Should I get a general travel card or an airline card?
A general travel card offers more flexibility because you can transfer points to multiple airline and hotel partners or redeem through a travel portal. An airline-specific card is better if you are loyal to one airline and want perks like free checked bags, priority boarding, and status upgrades. Many frequent travelers carry both types.
What is the best way to redeem travel points?
Transferring points to airline and hotel loyalty programs typically provides the highest value, often 1.5 to 3 cents per point compared to the standard 1 cent per point through a travel portal. Look for sweet spots in partner award charts, such as off-peak awards or premium cabin availability, to maximize your redemption value.
