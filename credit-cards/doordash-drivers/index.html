What to Know

How We Picked the Best Cards for Doordash Drivers

We compared annual fees, welcome bonuses, category earn rates, and fine print across every mainstream issuer, then filtered to cards whose bonus categories align with how doordash drivers typically spend. Every card on this list earns at least 2% effective cash-back return at realistic monthly spend.

Match the Card to Real Spending, Not the Marketing

The best card for doordash drivers is not the one with the flashiest welcome bonus -- it is the one that earns the most on your actual monthly spend. Pull up the last three months of statements, sum spend by category, and pick the card whose multiplier aligns with your biggest line items. If your spending is spread evenly, a flat-rate 2% card wins.

Responsible Use and Credit-Score Impact

Credit cards help your score when you pay the full balance every month and keep utilization below 30% of your limit. They hurt your score when you carry balances at 20%+ APR or miss payments. Set up autopay for at least the minimum on day one of the card to protect your payment history, then aim to pay the full statement balance each cycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for DoorDash drivers?
The Citi Custom Cash is the best credit card for DoorDash drivers because it automatically earns 5% cash back on your top spending category each billing cycle. Since gas is typically the biggest Dasher expense, you earn 5% on fuel with no activation required and no annual fee. This hands-off approach is ideal for drivers who are too busy delivering to manage rotating categories.
Can DoorDash drivers get a business credit card?
Yes. DoorDash drivers are independent contractors and can apply for business credit cards as sole proprietors. You do not need an LLC or formal business registration. Simply use your legal name as the business name and your Social Security number. Business cards help separate delivery expenses from personal spending for cleaner tax filing.
How much can DoorDash drivers save with credit card rewards?
A full-time Dasher spending $3,000-$5,000 per year on gas, $1,500-$2,500 on car maintenance, and $1,000 on meals between deliveries can earn $300-$700 per year in credit card rewards. Using a 5% gas card for fuel and a 3% dining card for meals maximizes total rewards across all delivery expenses.
What expenses can DoorDash drivers put on a credit card?
DoorDash drivers can charge gas, car maintenance, oil changes, tires, car washes, phone bills for the Dasher app, hot bags and delivery equipment, meals between deliveries, and car insurance to a credit card. All of these are deductible business expenses on Schedule C, and using a dedicated card simplifies tracking.
Should I use a separate card for DoorDash expenses?
Yes, using a dedicated credit card for all DoorDash expenses is strongly recommended. It simplifies tax preparation by keeping business and personal expenses separate. You can download the annual statement and use it directly for Schedule C deductions. This separation also makes it easier to calculate your true delivery profit after expenses.
What is the best card for new DoorDash drivers with limited credit?
The Discover it Cash Back is the best card for new Dashers with limited credit history. It accepts applicants with fair credit scores starting at 580 and offers a Cashback Match that doubles all rewards earned in the first year. This makes it an excellent starter card while you build credit and driving history.
Best card for DoorDash drivers who also do Uber Eats?
Drivers who deliver on multiple platforms should use the Wells Fargo Active Cash for its simple 2% on every purchase. Multi-app drivers have higher gas and maintenance costs, and the flat 2% rate rewards all delivery expenses equally without worrying about category caps or spending thresholds across different apps.
Do DoorDash drivers need to track credit card rewards for taxes?
Credit card rewards earned from personal purchases are generally not considered taxable income by the IRS. However, if you receive a sign-up bonus or referral bonus exceeding $600, the card issuer may issue a 1099-MISC. Consult a tax professional for guidance specific to your situation as a self-employed Dasher.
