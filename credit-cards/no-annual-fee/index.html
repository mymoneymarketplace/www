How to Choose a No Annual Fee Card

Key factors to consider before you apply.

1

Compare Rewards Rates

Just because a card has no annual fee does not mean the rewards are low. The best no-fee cards earn 2% to 5% in various categories. Look for a card whose highest earning rate matches your biggest spending category. A 2% flat-rate card is the safest choice if your spending is spread across many categories.

2

Check for Hidden Fees

While the annual fee is $0, other fees can add up. Check for foreign transaction fees if you travel internationally, balance transfer fees if you plan to move debt, and late payment penalties. Some no-fee cards also charge fees for cash advances or returned payments. Read the full fee schedule before applying.

3

Look at the Welcome Bonus

Many no annual fee cards offer welcome bonuses worth $150 to $200 with manageable spending requirements. Since there is no annual fee to offset, every dollar of the welcome bonus is pure profit. Factor the bonus value into your first-year comparison when deciding between cards.

No Annual Fee Card FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about no-fee cards.

Are no annual fee credit cards worth it?
Absolutely. Many no annual fee credit cards offer excellent rewards rates, welcome bonuses, and valuable perks. Cards like the Wells Fargo Active Cash earn 2% on all purchases and the Citi Custom Cash earns 5% on your top spending category, all without charging a yearly fee. These cards are especially valuable for people who want to earn rewards without worrying about whether their benefits justify a recurring cost.
What is the best no annual fee credit card?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash is widely considered the best no annual fee card for its flat 2% cash back on all purchases. For category earners, the Citi Custom Cash offers 5% on your top spending category automatically. The Wells Fargo Autograph provides 3x on six popular categories including dining, travel, and gas, making it a versatile no-fee option.
Do no annual fee cards have lower rewards?
Not necessarily. While some premium cards with annual fees offer higher rewards in certain categories, many no-fee cards compete effectively. The Wells Fargo Active Cash 2% flat rate matches or beats most fee-based cards on everyday spending. The Citi Custom Cash 5% rate on your top category is higher than many cards that charge annual fees.
Can I get a welcome bonus on a no annual fee card?
Yes, many no annual fee cards offer welcome bonuses worth $150 to $200 or more. For example, the Wells Fargo Active Cash offers a $200 bonus when you spend $500 in the first 3 months, and the Citi Custom Cash offers a similar bonus. These bonuses provide immediate value with no annual fee to offset them.
Do no annual fee cards have 0% intro APR?
Many no annual fee cards include 0% intro APR periods on purchases and balance transfers, typically lasting 12 to 15 months. The Wells Fargo Active Cash and Chase Freedom Unlimited both offer 15-month 0% intro APR periods on purchases. This makes them useful for financing large purchases interest-free while still earning rewards.
Should I close a no annual fee card I do not use?
Generally, you should keep no annual fee cards open even if you do not use them frequently. Open accounts contribute to your total available credit, which helps maintain a low credit utilization ratio and benefits your credit score. Since there is no annual fee, there is no cost to keeping the account open.
Do no annual fee cards have foreign transaction fees?
It depends on the card. Some no annual fee cards like the Discover it Cash Back and Wells Fargo Autograph charge no foreign transaction fee, while others like the Wells Fargo Active Cash and Citi Custom Cash charge 3% on purchases made outside the United States. Check the fee schedule before using any card for international travel.
How many no annual fee cards should I have?
There is no strict limit, but having two to three no annual fee cards with complementary bonus categories is a common strategy. For example, you might pair a flat 2% card for general spending with a 5% category card for your top expense and a 3x card for dining and travel. Since there are no annual fees, the only cost is managing multiple accounts responsibly.
