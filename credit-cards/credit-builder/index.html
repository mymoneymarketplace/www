How to Choose a Credit Builder Card

Key factors for building credit successfully.

1

Confirm Bureau Reporting

The entire purpose of a credit builder card is to establish a positive payment history on your credit report. Make sure the card reports to all three major bureaus: Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Reporting to only one or two bureaus means some lenders may not see your payment history when you apply for future credit products.

2

Keep Utilization Below 30%

Credit utilization, the percentage of your credit limit that you use, is a major factor in your credit score. If your secured card has a $300 limit, try to keep your balance below $90 at any time. Paying your balance before the statement closing date can help keep your reported utilization low even if you use the card frequently.

3

Look for an Upgrade Path

The best credit builder cards offer a path to graduate to an unsecured card after several months of responsible use. The Discover it Secured and Capital One Platinum Secured both review accounts for upgrade eligibility automatically. An upgrade returns your deposit and transitions you to a standard credit card without closing your account.

4

Avoid Cards with High Fees

Some credit builder cards charge high annual fees, monthly maintenance fees, or processing fees that eat into the limited credit available. Stick to cards with $0 annual fees like the Discover it Secured or Chime Credit Builder. Every dollar spent on fees is a dollar that could instead be used toward building your credit history responsibly.

Credit Builder Card FAQs

Common questions about building credit with cards.

What is the best credit builder card?
The Discover it Secured is the best credit builder card because it earns 2% cash back at restaurants and gas stations, matches all cash back earned in the first year, and reports to all three credit bureaus. It also has a path to upgrade to an unsecured card with responsible use.
How do secured credit cards work?
Secured credit cards require a refundable security deposit that typically equals your credit limit. You use the card like a regular credit card, and the issuer reports your payment activity to the credit bureaus. After several months of responsible use, many issuers will return your deposit and upgrade you to an unsecured card.
How long does it take to build credit with a secured card?
Most people see meaningful credit score improvements within six to twelve months of responsible secured card use. This means making small purchases, paying the full balance on time every month, and keeping utilization below 30%. Some cardholders are upgraded to unsecured cards within eight to twelve months.
Do credit builder cards report to all three bureaus?
Not all credit builder cards report to all three major bureaus. The Discover it Secured, Capital One Platinum Secured, and Self Secured Visa all report to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Always confirm that a card reports to all three bureaus before applying, as this is essential for building a complete credit profile.
Can I get a credit builder card with no credit history?
Yes, secured credit cards are specifically designed for people with no credit history. Since the security deposit reduces the issuer's risk, approval requirements are much lower than standard credit cards. Some cards like the OpenSky Secured Visa and Chime Credit Builder do not even check your credit during the application process.
What is the minimum deposit for a secured card?
Minimum deposits vary by card. The OpenSky Secured Visa requires a $200 minimum deposit. The Discover it Secured also has a $200 minimum. The Capital One Platinum Secured may approve you for a $200 credit line with a deposit as low as $49, depending on your creditworthiness.
Do I get my deposit back from a secured card?
Yes, your security deposit is fully refundable. You get it back when you close the account in good standing or when the issuer upgrades you to an unsecured card. Make sure your balance is paid off before closing the account, as the issuer may apply the deposit to any remaining balance.
Should I get a secured card or a credit builder loan?
A secured card is better for building credit through everyday purchases since it helps you develop the habit of using credit responsibly. A credit builder loan is better if you want to build credit through savings, as payments go into a savings account. Using both simultaneously can accelerate your credit building since you add multiple positive payment lines to your credit report.
