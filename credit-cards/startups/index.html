What to Know

How We Picked the Best Cards for Small Business Startups

We compared annual fees, welcome bonuses, category earn rates, and fine print across every mainstream issuer, then filtered to cards whose bonus categories align with how small business startups typically spend. Every card on this list earns at least 2% effective cash-back return at realistic monthly spend.

Match the Card to Real Spending, Not the Marketing

The best card for small business startups is not the one with the flashiest welcome bonus -- it is the one that earns the most on your actual monthly spend. Pull up the last three months of statements, sum spend by category, and pick the card whose multiplier aligns with your biggest line items. If your spending is spread evenly, a flat-rate 2% card wins.

Responsible Use and Credit-Score Impact

Credit cards help your score when you pay the full balance every month and keep utilization below 30% of your limit. They hurt your score when you carry balances at 20%+ APR or miss payments. Set up autopay for at least the minimum on day one of the card to protect your payment history, then aim to pay the full statement balance each cycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a sole proprietor get a business credit card?
Yes. Most business credit card issuers accept sole proprietors using their personal Social Security number and personal credit score. You do not need an EIN, LLC, or formal business registration. When applying, use your legal name as the business name and your personal income. Cards like the Ink Business Cash and Amex Blue Business Plus are commonly approved for sole proprietors with good personal credit.
What credit score do I need for a business credit card?
Most business credit cards require a personal FICO score of 670-680 or higher. Since new businesses have no business credit history, issuers evaluate the owner's personal creditworthiness. Some cards like the Ink Business Preferred require 680+, while the Brex card evaluates business cash balance instead of personal credit.
Do business credit cards build business credit?
Some business cards report to business credit bureaus like Dun and Bradstreet and Experian Business, which helps build your business credit profile. American Express business cards generally report to business bureaus, while Chase business cards typically do not report to personal credit bureaus (keeping your personal utilization clean). Check with each issuer about their reporting practices.
Should I use a personal or business credit card for my startup?
A business credit card is almost always better for startups because it separates personal and business expenses (critical for tax purposes and liability protection), often offers higher credit limits, provides employee cards, and may include business-specific benefits like expense tracking. Even sole proprietors benefit from the cleaner bookkeeping a business card provides.
What is the best no annual fee business card for startups?
The Ink Business Cash is the best no-annual-fee business card for startups because it earns 5% on office supplies, internet, and phone services, 2% on gas and restaurants, and 1% on everything else. These categories align perfectly with typical startup spending on software subscriptions, coworking space internet, and team lunches.
Can I get a business card with no revenue?
Yes. When applying for most business credit cards, you can list projected revenue or $0 revenue for a brand-new business. Issuers primarily evaluate your personal credit score, personal income, and existing debt. The Brex card is the exception -- it evaluates business bank account balance rather than personal credit or revenue history.
What is the best card for startup marketing expenses?
The Ink Business Preferred earns 3x points on online advertising, shipping, and internet services, making it the best card for startup marketing. If your startup spends heavily on Google Ads, Facebook Ads, or other digital marketing, the 3x rate on advertising purchases provides significant value. The 100,000-point welcome bonus also provides early capital.
Do I need an LLC to get a business credit card?
No. You do not need an LLC, corporation, or any formal business entity to apply for a business credit card. Sole proprietors can apply using their personal name as the business name and their Social Security number. However, forming an LLC provides liability protection that separates your personal assets from business debts, which is advisable as your startup grows.
