How to Choose a Rewards Card

Key factors to consider before you apply.

1

Match Rewards to Your Spending

Look at where you spend the most money each month. If dining is your biggest category, the Amex Gold with 4x at restaurants is hard to beat. If your spending is spread across many categories, a flat-rate card or one with broad bonus categories like the Wells Fargo Autograph is likely a better fit. The best rewards card is the one that earns the most on your actual purchases.

2

Value the Welcome Bonus

Welcome bonuses are often the most valuable feature of a new rewards card. A 60,000-point bonus from the Chase Sapphire Preferred can be worth $750 or more in travel. Calculate whether you can meet the minimum spending requirement with your normal purchases, and factor the bonus value into your first-year return when comparing cards.

3

Understand Redemption Options

Not all points are created equal. Transferable points from Chase, Amex, and Capital One can be worth 1.5 to 2+ cents each when transferred to airline or hotel partners. Fixed-value points are worth exactly 1 cent each regardless of how you redeem them. If you want maximum flexibility and value, choose a card with a strong transfer partner network.

Rewards Credit Card FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about rewards cards.

What is a rewards credit card?
A rewards credit card earns points, miles, or cash back on your purchases that can be redeemed for travel, statement credits, gift cards, merchandise, or other perks. The best rewards cards offer generous earning rates in popular spending categories, valuable welcome bonuses, and flexible redemption options that let you get the most value from your points.
What is the best rewards credit card right now?
The Chase Sapphire Preferred is the best overall rewards card for most people thanks to its 60,000-point welcome bonus, 3x earning on dining and travel, and the ability to transfer points to 14+ airline and hotel partners. For heavy dining and grocery spenders, the Amex Gold earns 4x in both categories. For a no-fee option, the Wells Fargo Autograph earns 3x on six categories.
What is the difference between points, miles, and cash back?
Cash back is the simplest reward, returned as a percentage of spending as a statement credit or deposit. Points are flexible currency that can be redeemed for travel, cash back, gift cards, or transferred to loyalty programs. Miles are similar to points but are typically associated with travel and airline programs. Points and miles can often deliver higher value when transferred to partners or redeemed for travel.
How do I maximize credit card rewards?
Use the right card for each spending category to earn the highest rate possible. Pay your balance in full every month to avoid interest charges that erode your rewards value. Take advantage of welcome bonuses by meeting the minimum spending requirement with your normal spending. Transfer points to airline and hotel partners for maximum redemption value rather than cashing out at lower rates.
Are rewards cards with annual fees worth it?
Rewards cards with annual fees are worth it when the total value of the rewards, credits, and perks exceeds the fee. For example, the Chase Sapphire Preferred has a $95 fee but its 60,000-point welcome bonus alone is worth $750 or more. Add in the ongoing earning rates, travel insurance, and purchase protection, and the value clearly outweighs the cost for active cardholders.
Can I have multiple rewards cards?
Yes, many people use a strategy of carrying two to four rewards cards to maximize earning across different spending categories. For example, you might use one card for dining, another for groceries, and a flat-rate card for everything else. Having multiple cards in the same points ecosystem, like Chase or Amex, lets you pool points for even higher redemption value.
What is a welcome bonus and how do I earn it?
A welcome bonus is a large lump-sum reward offered to new cardholders who meet a minimum spending requirement within a specified time frame, typically $3,000 to $5,000 in the first 3 months. Welcome bonuses can be worth $500 to $1,250 or more in value and are often the single most valuable feature of opening a new rewards card.
What are transfer partners and why do they matter?
Transfer partners are airline and hotel loyalty programs that accept point transfers from credit card rewards programs like Chase Ultimate Rewards, Amex Membership Rewards, and Capital One Miles. Transferring points to partners can increase their value from 1 cent to 2 cents or more per point. This flexibility is what makes transferable point cards the most valuable type of rewards card for frequent travelers.
