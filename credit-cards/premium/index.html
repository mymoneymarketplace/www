How to Choose a Premium Card

Key factors for selecting the right premium card.

1

Add Up Every Credit and Perk

Premium cards offer travel credits, airline incidentals, hotel credits, Global Entry reimbursement, and more. Create a list of every benefit the card provides and estimate how much you would realistically use. If the total value exceeds the annual fee by a comfortable margin, the card is likely worth the investment for your travel style.

2

Prioritize Lounge Access Networks

If airport lounges are important to you, compare the lounge networks carefully. The Amex Centurion Lounges are premium but limited in locations. Priority Pass offers the broadest network but quality varies. Capital One is building its own lounges. Choose the card whose lounge network aligns with the airports you use most often.

3

Match Earning Rates to Your Spending

Premium cards have different bonus category structures. The Amex Platinum earns 5x on flights but only 1x on most other spending. The Chase Sapphire Reserve earns 3x on travel and dining across the board. The Venture X earns 2x on everything. Choose the card whose bonus categories align with where you spend the most money.

Premium Card FAQs

Common questions about premium credit cards.

What is the best premium credit card?
The Amex Platinum is the best premium credit card for travelers who want Centurion Lounge access, 5x on flights, and extensive travel credits. The Chase Sapphire Reserve is the best alternative with a lower fee and $300 travel credit. The Capital One Venture X offers the best value at the $395 price point.
Are premium credit cards worth the high annual fee?
Premium cards are worth the fee if you travel frequently enough to use the benefits. The Amex Platinum's $695 fee is offset by over $1,500 in potential credits and perks for active travelers. If you take fewer than four to five trips per year, a mid-tier card may offer better net value.
What perks come with premium credit cards?
Premium cards typically include airport lounge access, annual travel credits, Global Entry or TSA PreCheck credits, hotel elite status, concierge service, travel insurance, purchase protection, and elevated earning rates on travel and dining purchases.
Do premium cards include airport lounge access?
Yes, most premium cards include some form of lounge access. The Amex Platinum provides Centurion Lounge and Priority Pass access. The Chase Sapphire Reserve and Capital One Venture X both include Priority Pass membership. Lounge access includes complimentary food, drinks, and Wi-Fi.
What credit score do I need for a premium card?
Premium credit cards typically require excellent credit, generally a score of 720 or higher. Some applicants with scores of 700 may be approved, but the best approval odds come with scores above 740. Issuers also consider income and existing relationship history.
What is the cheapest premium credit card?
The Citi Strata Premier at $95 per year is the most affordable premium card, offering 3x on travel, dining, and groceries. The Capital One Venture X at $395 offers the best value among higher-tier premium cards, with a $300 travel credit that effectively reduces the net cost to $95.
Can I have more than one premium card?
Yes, you can hold multiple premium cards from different issuers. Many travel enthusiasts pair the Amex Platinum with the Chase Sapphire Reserve to maximize lounge access networks and earning rates across different spending categories. Just make sure the combined fees are justified by the benefits you use.
How do I maximize the value of a premium card?
Use every available credit and perk each year, including travel credits, airline incidentals, hotel credits, and Global Entry reimbursement. Use the card for all travel and dining purchases to earn the highest rewards rate. Access lounges during every trip and take advantage of the concierge service for travel bookings and event tickets.
