What to Know

How We Picked the Best Cards for Pet Owners

We compared annual fees, welcome bonuses, category earn rates, and fine print across every mainstream issuer, then filtered to cards whose bonus categories align with how pet owners typically spend. Every card on this list earns at least 2% effective cash-back return at realistic monthly spend.

Match the Card to Real Spending, Not the Marketing

The best card for pet owners is not the one with the flashiest welcome bonus -- it is the one that earns the most on your actual monthly spend. Pull up the last three months of statements, sum spend by category, and pick the card whose multiplier aligns with your biggest line items. If your spending is spread evenly, a flat-rate 2% card wins.

Responsible Use and Credit-Score Impact

Credit cards help your score when you pay the full balance every month and keep utilization below 30% of your limit. They hurt your score when you carry balances at 20%+ APR or miss payments. Set up autopay for at least the minimum on day one of the card to protect your payment history, then aim to pay the full statement balance each cycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for vet bills?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash is the best card for vet bills because it earns 2% cash back on all veterinary expenses with no category restrictions. Vet bills can be unpredictable and expensive ($200-$5,000+), so a reliable 2% on every visit adds up. For emergency vet situations, the Discover it Cash Back offers a 0% intro APR that lets you pay off large vet bills over time without interest charges.
Can I earn 5% cash back on pet food?
Yes. The Amazon Prime Rewards Visa earns 5% on all Amazon and Chewy.com purchases, which includes pet food. The Blue Cash Preferred from Amex earns 6% on pet food purchased at U.S. supermarkets. The Citi Custom Cash automatically earns 5% on pet stores if that is your top monthly spending category.
What is the best card for Chewy orders?
The Amazon Prime Rewards Visa earns 5% on all Chewy.com orders since Chewy processes through the Amazon payment system. This makes it the highest-earning card for online pet supply purchases. If you already have Amazon Prime, there is no additional cost for the card.
Do any credit cards cover pet insurance?
No credit cards include pet insurance as a built-in benefit. However, you can earn cash back on pet insurance premium payments. The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on monthly pet insurance payments. Some cards offer purchase protection that may cover items bought for your pet, but this does not extend to veterinary care.
What is the best card for emergency vet visits?
The Discover it Cash Back is the best card for emergency vet financing because it offers a 0% intro APR on purchases, allowing you to pay off a large emergency vet bill over 15 months without interest. It also accepts applicants with fair credit (580+), making it accessible as a backup card specifically for pet emergencies.
Should I get a separate credit card for pet expenses?
A separate card for pet expenses can help you track spending and budget for your pets more effectively. It also provides a dedicated credit line for emergency vet visits. Consider a no-annual-fee card like the Wells Fargo Active Cash (2% on everything) or Discover it Cash Back (0% intro APR for emergencies) as a dedicated pet expense card.
What is the best card for pet grooming and boarding?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on grooming and boarding services since these are general service purchases. The Citi Custom Cash may earn 5% if pet services become your top monthly spending category. Grooming and boarding typically do not fall into bonus categories on most cards, making flat-rate cards the most reliable earners.
Can I earn rewards at PetSmart or Petco?
Yes. PetSmart and Petco purchases earn standard rewards on any credit card. The Citi Custom Cash can earn 5% at pet stores if that is your top monthly spending category. The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns a reliable 2% on all pet store purchases.
