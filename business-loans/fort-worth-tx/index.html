What to Know

How Fort Worth Businesses Access Capital

Small businesses in Fort Worth, Texas have multiple funding pathways. Traditional bank loans work for established businesses with 2+ years of financials and 680+ credit. SBA loans offer lower rates and longer terms but take 30-90 days. Online and alternative lenders approve in 24-72 hours with more flexible credit requirements, trading speed for slightly higher APR. The right fit depends on your time-in-business, revenue, and how fast you need the money.

Documents to Have Ready Before You Apply

Most lenders want: 3-6 months of business bank statements, the two most recent business tax returns, a current profit-and-loss statement, proof of business registration or articles of incorporation, a voided business check, and a photo ID for the signer. Having these uploaded before the application starts cuts the time-to-funding by several business days.

What to Look For Beyond the Headline Rate

Advertised APRs are only part of the cost. Always check: origination fees (typically 1-5% of the loan amount, built into APR on a true-APR quote), prepayment penalties (can trap you if you want to pay off early), daily or weekly repayment schedules (which compress cash flow), and personal guarantee requirements (standard for most small-business loans under $250K). A higher APR with no prepayment penalty is often cheaper than a lower APR with a 2% prepayment fee.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need for a business loan in Fort Worth, TX?
Credit score requirements vary by lender. SBA loans processed through the Dallas/Fort Worth District Office generally require 680+ personal credit. Texas-based banks (Frost, Cadence, PlainsCapital) typically want 650+ on conventional term loans. CDFIs like LiftFund and PeopleFund work with Fort Worth borrowers who have scores as low as 550, focusing on revenue, cash flow, and time in business rather than pure credit.
How does Texas Franchise Tax affect my Fort Worth business loan?
The Texas Franchise Tax applies only to businesses with total revenue above $2.47 million (the no-tax-due threshold). Below that, no franchise tax is due. Above the threshold, the rate is 0.375% for retail/wholesale and 0.75% for most other industries, computed on taxable margin. Fort Worth businesses with revenue under $2.47M face no state franchise tax, which strengthens cash flow profiles on lender projections. Texas also has no state income tax on operators.
How fast can I get a business loan approved in Fort Worth?
Approval speed varies by product. SBA 7(a) loans processed through the Dallas/Fort Worth District Office typically run 30-90 days. Fort Worth bank term loans average 2-4 weeks — Frost Bank and Cadence Bank are particularly active in Fort Worth SBA and conventional business lending. Online and alternative lenders deliver same-day decisions with funds in 1-3 business days.
What types of business loans are available in Fort Worth?
Fort Worth businesses access SBA 7(a) and 504 loans through the Dallas/Fort Worth District Office, Texas-headquartered regional bank financing (Frost, Cadence, PlainsCapital, Texas Capital), business lines of credit, equipment financing (heavy in Fort Worth's aerospace and manufacturing corridors), commercial real estate loans, invoice factoring, and merchant cash advances. CDFIs include LiftFund (based in San Antonio but active statewide) and PeopleFund.
Are there Fort Worth-specific resources for small business funding?
Yes. The Fort Worth SBDC at Tarrant County College provides free business advising and financing guidance. The Texas State Small Business Credit Initiative (SSBCI) guarantees qualified loans through participating lenders. The City of Fort Worth Economic Development Department administers local incentive programs. LiftFund's Fort Worth operations provide microloans to underserved entrepreneurs. TexasSourceLink connects startups to state and local funding resources.
