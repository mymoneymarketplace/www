What to Know

How Midland Businesses Access Capital

Small businesses in Midland, Texas have multiple funding pathways. Traditional bank loans work for established businesses with 2+ years of financials and 680+ credit. SBA loans offer lower rates and longer terms but take 30-90 days. Online and alternative lenders approve in 24-72 hours with more flexible credit requirements, trading speed for slightly higher APR. The right fit depends on your time-in-business, revenue, and how fast you need the money.

Documents to Have Ready Before You Apply

Most lenders want: 3-6 months of business bank statements, the two most recent business tax returns, a current profit-and-loss statement, proof of business registration or articles of incorporation, a voided business check, and a photo ID for the signer. Having these uploaded before the application starts cuts the time-to-funding by several business days.

What to Look For Beyond the Headline Rate

Advertised APRs are only part of the cost. Always check: origination fees (typically 1-5% of the loan amount, built into APR on a true-APR quote), prepayment penalties (can trap you if you want to pay off early), daily or weekly repayment schedules (which compress cash flow), and personal guarantee requirements (standard for most small-business loans under $250K). A higher APR with no prepayment penalty is often cheaper than a lower APR with a 2% prepayment fee.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do oil price cycles affect business loan availability in Midland, TX?
Oil price fluctuations directly impact lending in Midland. During boom periods, lenders are more willing to extend larger loans at favorable rates due to higher revenues across the local economy. During downturns, traditional banks may tighten requirements, but alternative lenders like Lendmate Capital continue to fund Permian Basin businesses based on current revenue and cash flow rather than commodity price forecasts. Diversifying your revenue streams strengthens your application regardless of the oil cycle.
Can oil field service companies in the Permian Basin get business loans?
Yes. Oil field service companies are among the most commonly funded businesses in Midland. Equipment financing, working capital lines, and term loans are all available for companies providing drilling, well servicing, transportation, and related oilfield operations. Lenders familiar with the Permian Basin understand the capital-intensive nature of these businesses and structure funding accordingly, with loan amounts ranging from $25,000 to $5 million.
What documents do I need to apply for a business loan in Midland, TX?
Most Midland business loan applications require 3 to 12 months of business bank statements, your Texas LLC or corporate filing from the Secretary of State, an EIN from the IRS, a valid Texas Sales Tax Permit if applicable, and recent tax returns. Some alternative lenders only require 3 months of bank statements and a simple one-page application, making the process significantly faster than traditional bank loans.
Are there local programs to help Midland small businesses get funding?
Midland has several local and regional resources. The Midland Development Corporation offers economic incentives for business growth. LiftFund provides microloans and small business loans across West Texas. The UTPB Small Business Development Center offers free counseling and loan application assistance. SCORE Permian Basin provides mentoring, and the SBA Lubbock District Office covers the Midland-Odessa area for federal loan programs.
How does Midland's boom-and-bust economy affect my loan terms?
Lenders assess risk differently depending on economic conditions. During boom periods, Midland businesses often qualify for larger loan amounts and more favorable rates due to strong revenues. During bust cycles, lenders may require more collateral or shorter repayment terms. The key is demonstrating consistent cash flow and a business model that can weather price swings. Alternative lenders tend to be more flexible than traditional banks during economic downturns in the Permian Basin.
