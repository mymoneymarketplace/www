What to Know

How Bellevue Businesses Access Capital

Small businesses in Bellevue, Washington have multiple funding pathways. Traditional bank loans work for established businesses with 2+ years of financials and 680+ credit. SBA loans offer lower rates and longer terms but take 30-90 days. Online and alternative lenders approve in 24-72 hours with more flexible credit requirements, trading speed for slightly higher APR. The right fit depends on your time-in-business, revenue, and how fast you need the money.

Documents to Have Ready Before You Apply

Most lenders want: 3-6 months of business bank statements, the two most recent business tax returns, a current profit-and-loss statement, proof of business registration or articles of incorporation, a voided business check, and a photo ID for the signer. Having these uploaded before the application starts cuts the time-to-funding by several business days.

What to Look For Beyond the Headline Rate

Advertised APRs are only part of the cost. Always check: origination fees (typically 1-5% of the loan amount, built into APR on a true-APR quote), prepayment penalties (can trap you if you want to pay off early), daily or weekly repayment schedules (which compress cash flow), and personal guarantee requirements (standard for most small-business loans under $250K). A higher APR with no prepayment penalty is often cheaper than a lower APR with a 2% prepayment fee.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Washington's lack of state income tax affect business loans in Bellevue?
Washington has no personal or corporate state income tax, which means Bellevue business owners keep more of their revenue. Lenders view this favorably because it improves your effective cash flow and debt-service coverage ratio. However, Washington does impose a Business & Occupation (B&O) Tax on gross receipts, so lenders will factor that obligation into your application. The net effect is generally positive—Bellevue businesses often qualify for larger loan amounts compared to similar businesses in high-income-tax states.
What is the WA B&O Tax and how does it impact my loan application?
The Washington Business & Occupation (B&O) Tax is a gross receipts tax applied to nearly all businesses operating in the state. Rates vary by business classification—retailing at 0.471%, services at 1.5%, and manufacturing at 0.484%. Unlike income tax, B&O Tax is assessed on gross revenue regardless of profitability. Lenders familiar with Washington businesses account for this when underwriting loans. Having your B&O Tax filings current with the Department of Revenue strengthens your application and demonstrates fiscal responsibility.
Can Bellevue tech startups and contractors get business loans?
Yes. Bellevue's Eastside tech ecosystem is one of the strongest in the country, and lenders are very familiar with funding tech companies, IT consultancies, and independent contractors in this market. Startups with at least 6 months of revenue history and $10,000 or more in monthly deposits can qualify with alternative lenders. SBA loans typically require 2+ years of operating history. Many Bellevue tech professionals transitioning from companies like Microsoft, Amazon, or T-Mobile into their own ventures qualify for startup-friendly funding products.
What documents do I need to apply for a business loan in Bellevue, WA?
Most lenders require 3 to 12 months of business bank statements, a valid EIN, your Washington LLC or corporation registration with the WA Secretary of State, an active Bellevue business license, and a government-issued ID. SBA loans require additional documentation including federal tax returns, a personal financial statement, and a business plan. Because Washington has no state income tax return, lenders rely more heavily on bank statements and federal returns to verify revenue.
Are there local programs to help Bellevue small businesses get funding?
Bellevue and the greater Eastside offer several resources. The Bellevue Chamber of Commerce connects businesses with lending partners and financial workshops. Craft3 is a Pacific Northwest CDFI that provides loans from $25,000 to $2 million to businesses that may not qualify for traditional bank financing. The Washington SBDC at Bellevue College offers free business advising and loan application assistance. SCORE Eastside provides free mentorship from experienced business professionals. The SBA Seattle District Office supports the entire Puget Sound region with loan guarantees and small business resources.
