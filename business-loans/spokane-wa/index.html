What to Know

How Spokane Businesses Access Capital

Small businesses in Spokane, Washington have multiple funding pathways. Traditional bank loans work for established businesses with 2+ years of financials and 680+ credit. SBA loans offer lower rates and longer terms but take 30-90 days. Online and alternative lenders approve in 24-72 hours with more flexible credit requirements, trading speed for slightly higher APR. The right fit depends on your time-in-business, revenue, and how fast you need the money.

Documents to Have Ready Before You Apply

Most lenders want: 3-6 months of business bank statements, the two most recent business tax returns, a current profit-and-loss statement, proof of business registration or articles of incorporation, a voided business check, and a photo ID for the signer. Having these uploaded before the application starts cuts the time-to-funding by several business days.

What to Look For Beyond the Headline Rate

Advertised APRs are only part of the cost. Always check: origination fees (typically 1-5% of the loan amount, built into APR on a true-APR quote), prepayment penalties (can trap you if you want to pay off early), daily or weekly repayment schedules (which compress cash flow), and personal guarantee requirements (standard for most small-business loans under $250K). A higher APR with no prepayment penalty is often cheaper than a lower APR with a 2% prepayment fee.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to pay state income tax on my Spokane business loan proceeds?
No. Washington State has no personal or corporate income tax, which is a significant advantage for Spokane business owners. However, most businesses are subject to Washington's Business & Occupation (B&O) Tax, which is a gross receipts tax. Loan proceeds themselves are not taxable income, but the revenue generated from deploying that capital may be subject to B&O Tax. Consult a local tax advisor for your specific situation.
How does Spokane's cost of doing business compare to Seattle for loan qualification?
Spokane's cost of doing business is significantly lower than Seattle's. Commercial lease rates in Spokane average $12–$22 per square foot compared to $35–$55 in Seattle. This means Spokane businesses often have healthier margins and lower overhead, which lenders view favorably during underwriting. Many Spokane businesses qualify for larger loan amounts relative to their revenue because of these lower operating costs.
What documents do I need to apply for a business loan in Spokane, WA?
Most lenders require 3–12 months of business bank statements, your WA LLC or corporate registration filed with the Washington Secretary of State, an EIN from the IRS, a valid Spokane business license, and recent tax returns. Alternative lenders like Lendmate Capital may only require 3 months of bank statements and basic business information, with approvals in as little as 24 hours.
Are there business loan programs specifically for Spokane-area veterans and military families?
Yes. Spokane's proximity to Fairchild Air Force Base means the region has a strong veteran business community. The SBA offers Veterans Advantage loans with reduced fees. SCORE Spokane provides free mentorship for veteran entrepreneurs. Additionally, the Washington State Department of Veterans Affairs connects business owners with state-specific resources and the Boots to Business program helps transitioning service members access startup capital.
Can a Spokane startup with less than one year in business get a loan?
Yes, though options are more limited for newer businesses. Alternative lenders may fund Spokane startups with as few as 3–6 months of revenue history. Craft3, a Pacific Northwest CDFI, offers community development loans that are more accessible to early-stage businesses. The WA Small Business Development Center at Eastern Washington University also helps startups prepare stronger applications and connect with appropriate funding sources.
